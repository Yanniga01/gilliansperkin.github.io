Semrush has been the name most SEO professionals reach for when someone asks "what tool do you use?" That reputation didn't happen by accident — but it also hasn't gone unchallenged. This review doesn't just list features. It tells you what those features actually do, what they cost in practice, and whether the whole package is worth it for your specific situation.

What's New in 2026: Semrush One Changes Everything

The most important thing to understand about Semrush in 2026 is that the platform has fundamentally repositioned itself. It is no longer just an SEO tool.

Semrush One is the company's significant, future-focused upgrade — combining its classic SEO toolkit with a new suite of AI visibility features, giving you a unified view of how your brand appears across both traditional search results and emerging AI-driven environments .

Why does that matter? Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Gemini are reshaping how answers are presented. The battle for visibility has shifted from ranking on page one to being cited inside AI-generated responses .

Semrush One was announced at the Semrush Spotlight conference in October 2025 , and it's now the flagship product. Understanding how it differs from the classic plans is essential before evaluating whether Semrush is worth it for your needs.

Semrush Features: What You Actually Get

Core SEO Toolkit (Available on All Plans)

The SEO Pro plan gives you up to 5 projects, 500 keywords tracked daily, and the ability to audit up to 100,000 pages of your website per month. You get access to the Keyword Magic Tool and detailed backlink analytics .

The biggest limitation at the Pro level is access to the Content Marketing Platform — you can find keywords, but you don't get Semrush's guidance on how to build content around them. It's more of a research tool than an execution tool at this level .

The Guru plan resolves that. It unlocks the full Content Marketing Platform and access to historical data going back 12 months — two features that meaningfully separate it from Pro. For content-driven sites and agencies managing multiple clients, Guru is where Semrush starts to feel like a complete toolkit.

The AI Visibility Toolkit (New in Semrush One)

This is the genuinely new territory. The AI Visibility Toolkit now tracks how your brand appears in Large Language Models — monitoring brand mentions and sentiment across ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, and soon Gemini. In one workflow, you can track a keyword, see who ranks for it, write content to target it, and now track whether AI models cite that content .

One feature that stands out in particular: Semrush's data can surface a critical "mention-source divide" — fewer than 1 in 5 brands are both mentioned in AI answers and cited as authoritative sources. In other words, AI tools might know your brand exists without ever sending users to your site. That gap is now measurable for the first time.

Site audits now include AI crawlability and AI readiness checks, highlighting pages that are technically fine for Google but confusing for AI systems . This is the kind of insight that simply didn't exist in any mainstream SEO tool before 2025.

Semrush treats prompt research the same way it treats keyword research — search for a prompt in your industry and it will show you AI volume, topic difficulty, search intent, competing brand mentions, and related topics. Prompts are increasingly the new keywords, and this is one of the first tools to quantify them.

What's Still Behind Extra Paywalls

Here's a frustration that doesn't get enough airtime in reviews: despite Semrush One being considerably more expensive than the classic SEO plans, it still keeps several sophisticated tools behind separate paywalls — including Traffic & Market Analytics, the Social Media Toolkit, and the Advertising Toolkit, all only available as paid add-ons .

Costs can climb fast as you scale, and ROI can be hard to justify — particularly because the packaging makes it difficult to forecast what scaling will actually cost before committing .

The Hidden Cost Nobody Budgets For: Extra Users

All Semrush plans — including Semrush One — come with just one user account. Additional seats cost between $45 and $100 per month depending on your plan. For agencies or teams where multiple people need access, this cost compounds quickly and rarely gets factored into initial budget comparisons.

Enterprise Pricing

The Semrush Enterprise platform is an entirely separate solution from the core Semrush platform — designed for organizations like Samsung, Salesforce, and SAP. It adds a new dashboard with enterprise-level SEO and automation tools, access to vetted SEO experts, and extensive reporting and automation features. Pricing is fully customized .

Semrush Pros & Cons

✅ What Semrush Does Better Than Anyone

  • Database depth. Semrush's database holds more than 26.4 billion keywords, compared to Moz's 1.25 billion . At this scale, the difference shows up in the gaps you find during keyword research.
  • AI visibility as a workflow. Semrush One is strongest as a planning and monitoring tool. The prompts and competitor gaps make it easier to pick content targets without guessing .
  • Practical competitor intelligence. The Competitor Insights panel highlights specific topic clusters where your dominance is slipping .
  • The breadth is unmatched. Semrush is the gold standard for generalist SEO teams .

❌ Where Semrush Falls Short

  • AI coverage has gaps. Semrush One tracks your brand across ChatGPT and Google AI Mode but doesn't currently cover Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, or Meta AI .
  • The methodology is opaque. The dashboard displays numbers that look precise — visibility percentages, sentiment graphs — but the process behind those numbers remains mostly opaque .
  • It's expensive to get the full picture. The best part about using Semrush is that it can act as an all-in-one marketing platform — but Semrush's classic SEO plans begin at $139.95/month, which you pay in addition to the AI tool add-on if you're not on Semrush One .
  • Not optimized for pure GEO execution. For brand leads and GEO strategists, the distinction comes down to measurement vs. optimization. Semrush excels at measuring visibility. Specialized GEO platforms focus on the tactical workflows .

Is Semrush Worth It in 2026?

That depends almost entirely on who's asking.

✅ Yes, it's worth it if you're:

  • An agency or in-house team managing multiple websites with serious SEO budgets
  • A content-driven brand that needs the full stack — keyword research, audits, content tools, and now AI visibility — in one place
  • Already using Semrush Classic and wondering whether Semrush One is worth the upgrade (short answer: if AI search is part of your strategy, yes)

❌ No, it's hard to justify if you're:

  • A freelancer or small business doing basic SEO — for freelancers, bloggers, and small businesses, the cost is difficult to justify when alternatives cover 80% of the feature set at 30% of the price
  • A team of 3+ people who will need multiple seats — the per-seat add-on cost changes the math significantly
  • Primarily focused on influencing AI outputs rather than monitoring them (in which case a specialized GEO tool may serve you better)

The honest summary: Semrush One should be in your possession if you publish content regularly, watch rankings, care about link building, and make decisions based on search data — especially if you or your customers are asking questions to ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity as often as you search on Google . For everyone else, the classic Pro or Guru plans still represent solid, proven value — just don't expect the AI visibility layer without paying for it.

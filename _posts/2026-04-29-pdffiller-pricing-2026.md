<!-- The Hidden Math: Monthly vs Annual -->
<section id="hidden-math" class="content-section">
  <h2>🧮 The "Hidden" Math: Monthly vs. Annual Billing</h2>
  <p>Neil Patel often highlights that “most marketing metrics are misleading”. The same applies to SaaS pricing. PDFfiller advertises their low annual rates, but if you choose the monthly “no‑commitment” route, your costs skyrocket.</p>
  <p><strong>Real example:</strong> The Basic plan is <strong>$20/month</strong> on monthly billing – that’s <strong>$240/year</strong>. The annual plan is <strong>$96/year</strong> ($8/month). You save <strong>$144 – a 60% reduction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Pro Tip:</strong> If you plan to use the tool for more than 4 months, choose the annual plan. The monthly premium is essentially a convenience tax that will cost you double over a year.</p>
</section>

<!-- The 30‑Day Trial Trap -->
<section id="trial-trap" class="content-section">
  <h2>⚠️ Navigating the 30‑Day Free Trial: Avoid the "Trap"</h2>
  <p>This is where transparency is vital. According to our independent research, billing concerns are the #1 source of user frustration.</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>The Auto‑Renew:</strong> The trial requires a credit card upfront.</li>
    <li><strong>The Charge:</strong> If you don’t cancel by Day 29, you are charged the full annual amount (up to $180) in one lump sum.</li>
    <li><strong>The Recovery:</strong> You have 180 days to request a refund, but approval depends on how much you’ve used the tool.</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>Expert Advice:</strong> Set a calendar reminder for Day 27 and screenshot your cancellation confirmation.</p>
</section>

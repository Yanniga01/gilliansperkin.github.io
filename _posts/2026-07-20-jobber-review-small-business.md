<h2 id="what-is-jobber">🔍 What Jobber Actually Is</h2>
<p>Jobber launched in 2011 in Edmonton, Canada, with a clear mission: build field service software that small businesses can actually use without a dedicated IT team or a six‑month implementation process. Most field service management software requires genuine configuration expertise to unlock its value. Jobber works on day one.</p>

<p>At its core, <strong>Jobber is an all‑in‑one, cloud‑based field service management platform</strong> specifically designed for small to mid‑sized home service businesses – landscaping, HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, electrical, pest control, roofing, carpentry, and dozens of similar trades.</p>

<p>The platform handles quoting, scheduling, dispatching, client communication, invoicing, and online payment collection in one place. The <strong>Client Hub</strong> lets customers approve quotes, track appointment times, and pay invoices without calling the office. GPS tracking shows where technicians are in real time. The mobile app works offline and syncs when connectivity returns.</p>

<p>A total of <strong>78% of Jobber reviewers have between 2 and 50 employees</strong> – which tells you exactly who the platform is built for and who gets the most from it.</p>

<div style="background: #f0f7ff; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 12px; margin: 1.5rem 0;">
    <p style="font-style: italic; font-size: 1rem;">"Most field service businesses reach a point where spreadsheets, text messages, handwritten invoices, and separate payment apps stop being manageable. The chaos isn't sudden — it accumulates over months until one missed appointment or one unpaid invoice finally makes the cost of disorganization visible. Jobber exists for that moment."</p>
</div>

<p>Before getting into features and pricing, this is the most useful framing for the whole review.</p>

<p><strong>Jobber is purpose‑built for residential home service businesses.</strong> Jobber is built for home service businesses, primarily small businesses, which make up <strong>97% of reviewers</strong>, especially in construction at 40% and consumer services at 22%.</p>

<p>If you run a landscaping company, an HVAC operation, a cleaning service, a plumbing business, or any trade that involves scheduling crews, visiting client properties, and billing for completed work – Jobber was designed around your specific workflow.</p>

<p>It works best for trade and home service operations with active crews and high job volumes, where efficient scheduling and fast invoice turnaround directly impact cash flow.</p>

<h3>❌ Who it doesn't serve well:</h3>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Commercial‑only contractors</strong> – businesses with complex multi‑phase project billing beyond straightforward field service work</li>
    <li><strong>Enterprises</strong> that need the kind of deep reporting and ERP integration that platforms like ServiceTitan provide</li>
    <li><strong>Businesses with more than 15 employees</strong> – where the per‑user pricing model becomes inefficient</li>
</ul>
<p>Jobber has <strong>limited use among midsize and enterprise companies at 1% each</strong> – not because it's inadequate, but because it's genuinely designed for a different scale of operation.</p>

<!-- Core Features -->
<h2 id="features">🔑 Core Features: What You Actually Get</h2>

<h3>📅 Scheduling and Dispatching</h3>
<p>Scheduling is the feature most Jobber users interact with daily, and it's where the platform earns its strongest consistent praise.</p>
<p>The <strong>drag‑and‑drop dispatcher</strong> lets you assign jobs to crew members visually, see their existing schedule for the day, and reorder assignments when something changes – without text chains and manual coordination.</p>
<p><strong>Route optimization</strong> shows the most efficient sequence for visiting multiple job sites in a single day, reducing drive time and fuel costs. For a landscaping company running multiple crews across a metro area, this pays for the subscription in saved hours within the first month.</p>
<p>The <strong>automated reminder system</strong> sends appointment confirmations and reminders to clients via email or SMS without manual follow‑up from the office – the kind of automation that reduces no‑shows without adding administrative time.</p>

<h3>📄 Quoting and Estimates</h3>
<p>Businesses can produce <strong>professional and branded proposals</strong> tailored to their style, enhancing client presentations and boosting conversion rates.</p>
<p>The quoting workflow is designed to move fast: build a quote from saved line items, attach photos from the initial assessment, set optional add‑ons the client can choose, and send it via email with a link for online approval. The client signs off digitally, the approved quote converts to a job automatically, and the job appears in the schedule – no re‑entering data between systems.</p>
<p>The <strong>optional line items</strong> feature is worth highlighting for upselling. Including add‑on services as checkboxes the client can optionally select – gutter cleaning alongside a roof inspection, a spring tune‑up alongside an HVAC service – is a systematic way to increase average job value without a sales conversation.</p>

<h3>💰 Invoicing and Payment Collection</h3>
<p>This is where the operational impact of Jobber is most directly measurable for small businesses.</p>
<p>Users report <strong>dramatically faster payment turnaround</strong> after switching to Jobber's invoicing workflow – a consistent theme across independent reviews. The mechanism is straightforward: jobs convert to invoices automatically upon completion, invoices go out the same day via email or SMS with an online payment link, and clients can pay immediately from their phone rather than waiting to receive and mail a check.</p>
<p><strong>Two features added in late 2025</strong> expand invoicing flexibility significantly:</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Tap to Pay</strong> (launched November 2025) – lets you accept contactless payments through the mobile app without extra hardware</li>
    <li><strong>Progress Invoicing</strong> (launched November 2025) – enables splitting large jobs into milestone‑based billing</li>
</ul>
<p>The progress invoicing addition specifically addresses a common pain point for larger projects – landscaping installations, HVAC system replacements – where requiring full payment at job completion puts cash flow pressure on the client and reduces conversion. Milestone billing resolves that without needing a separate billing tool.</p>

<h3>🏠 Client Hub</h3>
<p>The Client Hub is the customer‑facing portal that sets Jobber apart from simpler field service tools. Clients get a personalized link – <strong>no login required</strong> – where they can see upcoming appointment times, review and approve quotes, pay invoices, and see their service history.</p>
<p>For a small service business competing against larger franchises, this level of professionalism matters more than most owners initially realize. A client who can check their appointment time, see their technician's route on the day of service, and pay their invoice from their phone has a meaningfully different experience than one who has to call the office for any information.</p>

<h3>📱 Mobile App for Field Teams</h3>
<p><strong>Excellent mobile apps that field teams actually want to use</strong> is a phrase that surfaces consistently in independent reviews – and it's worth taking seriously, because the best field service software on the desktop is useless if the crew doesn't use the app.</p>
<p>Jobber's iOS and Android apps let technicians:</p>
<ul>
    <li>See their schedule for the day</li>
    <li>Navigate to job sites</li>
    <li>Complete job checklists</li>
    <li>Add notes and photos</li>
    <li>Collect client signatures on‑site</li>
    <li>Process payments – all from their phone</li>
</ul>
<p>The <strong>offline mode</strong> is a practical feature for crews working in areas with unreliable connectivity: the app functions normally offline and syncs when a connection is available.</p>
<p>The interface is genuinely clean – a design choice that directly affects adoption. Apps that field workers find confusing or slow get abandoned in favor of text messages and paper notes. Jobber's mobile UX is consistently praised specifically because it doesn't feel designed by people who don't do field work.</p>

<h3>📍 GPS Tracking</h3>
<p>Real‑time GPS tracking of field technicians is available on higher‑tier plans, showing where each crew member is during the workday from the dispatcher's view. For dispatching decisions, client questions about arrival times, and accountability across larger crews, this eliminates the need to call technicians to get location information.</p>

<h3>🔗 Integrations</h3>
<p><strong>Compatibility with platforms like QuickBooks</strong> eases bookkeeping tasks, reducing manual entry and minimizing errors. The QuickBooks integration is the most‑used and most‑praised integration Jobber offers – invoices, payments, and expense data flow between the platforms without manual re‑entry.</p>
<p>Additional integrations include:</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Stripe</strong> – payment processing</li>
    <li><strong>Mailchimp</strong> – email marketing</li>
    <li><strong>Google suite</strong> – Calendar, Drive, Local Services Ads</li>
</ul>
<p>For most small field service businesses, these cover the full integration need without requiring API configuration or middleware tools.</p>

<h3>🤖 The Jobber AI Assistant</h3>
<p>Jobber's 2026 AI features include an in‑app assistant for drafting client messages, generating quote descriptions, and suggesting follow‑up actions based on job history. The AI isn't replacing the workflow – it's accelerating the communication tasks that take time but don't require craft.</p>
<p>Writing a post‑service follow‑up email, generating a professional description for an estimate line item, or drafting a response to a client inquiry are all faster with AI assistance in the workflow.</p>
<p>For a solo operator who handles customer communication personally alongside field work, this time saving compounds meaningfully across a week.</p>

<h3>What Each Tier Means for Your Small Business</h3>
<p><strong>Core – $29–$39/month (1 user)</strong><br>
The Core plan is the right starting point for solo operators – a single technician or owner‑operator who needs quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and basic client management in one place without paying for features a one‑person operation doesn't need. You get the Client Hub, the mobile app, and basic job management.</p>

<p><strong>Connect – $89–$119/month Individual, $169–$199/month Teams</strong><br>
Connect is where Jobber becomes the operational backbone of a real small business. Automated client reminders, two‑way client messaging, online booking, expense tracking, GPS tracking, and QuickBooks sync are all included. For most small field service businesses with a crew of two to five people, <strong>Connect covers every daily operational need</strong>.</p>
<p>The Teams version at $169/month for five users works out to <strong>$33.80 per user</strong> – a reasonable per‑seat cost for the operational efficiency gains.</p>

<p><strong>Grow – $149–$199/month Individual, $299–$349/month Teams</strong><br>
Grow adds the Marketing Suite, lead management, automated quote follow‑ups, detailed job costing reports, and advanced automation. The additional automation features at this tier – follow‑up sequences that run without manual triggering – are where Jobber starts to replace a part‑time administrative role for growing businesses.</p>

<p><strong>Plus Teams – $449–$599/month (15 users)</strong><br>
The Plus tier adds call recording, a dedicated account manager, and priority support. At this price point and team size, the question of whether Jobber or a more enterprise‑oriented platform like ServiceTitan makes better sense deserves honest evaluation.</p>

<h3>Marketing Suite Add‑On – $79/month</h3>
<p>The Marketing Suite is an add‑on designed to help you attract new customers and grow your business. It offers:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Google review management and automatic review requests</li>
    <li>A referral program</li>

</ul>
<p>This add‑on is worth calculating specifically against the alternatives. If you're currently paying for a separate email marketing tool, a review management platform, and a website builder, the <strong>$79/month consolidation may reduce your total software spend</strong> rather than increase it.</p>

<h2>🚀 New Features That Change the 2026 Evaluation</h2>
<p>Two features launched in November 2025 meaningfully change how Jobber fits into certain workflows:</p>

<p><strong>Tap to Pay</strong> removes the last hardware requirement for mobile payment collection. Previously, accepting card payments on‑site required a card reader accessory. Now, a technician can accept contactless payments (tap, Apple Pay, Google Pay) directly through the Jobber app on any compatible iPhone or Android device. For smaller operations that want to avoid equipment costs and the complication of managing card reader hardware across multiple crew members, this is a genuine workflow simplification.</p>

<p><strong>Progress Invoicing</strong> opens Jobber to larger project work that previously didn't fit its billing model cleanly. Splitting a $12,000 landscaping renovation into a deposit, a midpoint milestone, and a completion payment – all managed within the same Jobber job – means the platform now handles project‑based billing alongside the service call model it was originally built around. This expands the business types where Jobber is a good fit.</p>

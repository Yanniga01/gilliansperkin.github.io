How Campaigner's Pricing Model Works

Before diving into specific numbers, it's worth understanding the logic behind Campaigner's pricing structure — because it's more layered than most email tools.

Campaigner uses a subscriber-based pricing model, meaning your monthly cost scales with the size of your contact list, not the number of emails you send. This is meaningful: if you're a business that sends frequent campaigns to a large list, you won't face overage charges just for emailing more. That's in contrast to send-volume models (used by some tools) where a busy promotional month can unexpectedly spike your bill.

Every plan — Starter, Essential, and Advanced — can be billed monthly or annually, and choosing annual saves you 18%. That's worth factoring in early. On the Essential plan ($179/month), annual billing saves you roughly $387 over the year.

The Add-Ons You Need to Know About

This is where the "hidden costs" conversation starts.

SMS Add-On for Starter Plan

If you're on the Starter plan and want SMS, the SMS bundle can be added to any tier for an additional $45 per month. It includes a free phone number, up to 1,000 monthly SMS sends, and features that mirror Campaigner's email campaign tools — including recurring campaigns, personalization, and segmentation. That's a significant jump on a $59/month plan — effectively increasing your cost by 76% if you add it. Know this going in.

Geographic Limit on SMS

SMS is only available for US and Canadian numbers as of early 2026. International businesses or those targeting audiences in the UK, Australia, or Europe cannot use the SMS feature at all. This isn't prominently stated during signup, and it's a dealbreaker for non-North American teams.

The Credit Card Trial Requirement

Campaigner offers a free 30-day trial, but requires a credit card upfront. Your card is only charged if you choose a plan after the trial — however, most email marketing solutions do not require a credit card for a free trial at all. This is a common complaint across review platforms. The trial auto-converts to a paid plan if you don't actively cancel — which means the responsibility is on you to opt out, not opt in. Set a calendar reminder for day 27.

No Free Plan — Ever

Unlike many competitors, Campaigner does not offer a free version of the software. If your list is under 500 contacts and you're budget-conscious, Campaigner is genuinely not the right starting point. Tools like Mailchimp, MailerLite, or Brevo all have functional free tiers for early-stage senders.

The Annual Billing Risk

One concern that surfaces consistently in user reviews across Capterra and G2 is the billing behavior around annual plans. Some users have reported difficulty canceling and being billed beyond the period they intended to use. Campaigner's cancellation process requires a phone call — not just clicking a button in a dashboard — as the company verifies account ownership and the identity of the card holder before processing any cancellation.

This is documented company policy, not a glitch. But it's something to be aware of: if you sign up for an annual plan and your team changes or your needs shift mid-year, getting out early isn't as frictionless as it is with monthly tools. Always read the cancellation terms before committing to an annual contract.

⚠️ Key Takeaway

Campaigner's pricing is transparent at the top level but has several traps worth knowing: credit card trial requirement, phone-call cancellation, SMS geographic restriction, and the steep jump between Starter and Essential with no middle tier.

What $179/Month Actually Gets You on Essential

The Essential plan is where most growing businesses land, so it's worth getting specific about what's included vs. what you might assume is included:

✅ Included

  • 1,000 SMS sends/month with free phone number
  • Personalized content, segmentation, geolocation targeting
  • Recurring SMS campaigns
  • Dynamic content blocks
  • Multivariate testing beyond basic A/B
  • Conversion and revenue tracking

❌ Not Included

  • Dedicated account manager (Advanced and above)
  • Phone support (lower tiers get email and chat only)
  • More than 25,000 contacts without upgrading

If your list sits between 5,000 and 25,000 contacts and you need SMS in the same platform, Essential is well-priced relative to alternatives. The same feature set on ActiveCampaign or HubSpot runs meaningfully higher at comparable contact counts.

Who Gets the Best Value at Each Price Point

Starter ($59/mo)

A reasonable pick if you have under 5,000 contacts, don't need SMS, and want access to 900+ templates and solid automation without paying more. The caveat: if you grow past 5,000 contacts, you're immediately jumping to $179/month — there's no mid-tier.

Essential ($179/mo)

The sweet spot for most mid-sized businesses. You're getting SMS, multivariate testing, dynamic content, and 25,000 contacts — features that cost considerably more elsewhere.

Advanced ($649/mo)

Only makes sense at 100,000+ contacts or if your team needs the higher SMS send limits. Most businesses will never need this tier.

eCommerce ($79.95–$149.95/mo)

The outlier: unlimited contacts at a starting price that's lower than the Starter plan makes it the best value in Campaigner's lineup for Shopify and Magento store owners specifically. If that's your setup, this plan should be your first consideration, not an afterthought.

The Honest Verdict on Pricing

Campaigner's pricing is transparent at the top level but has a few traps worth knowing in advance: the credit card trial requirement, the phone-call cancellation process, the SMS geographic restriction, and the steep jump between Starter and Essential with no middle tier.

That said, the core value proposition holds up. Email and contact limits vary by pricing tier, with higher plans offering expanded contact lists and increased sending volumes to accommodate growth — and for mid-market teams, the Essential plan's combination of features relative to price is genuinely competitive in 2026.

If you're comparing tools primarily on cost, Campaigner won't always win. But if you're comparing on cost per feature — especially once you factor in SMS, multivariate testing, and segmentation depth — it holds its ground well against tools charging twice as much.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Campaigner have a free plan? ▼

No, Campaigner does not offer a free plan. They offer a 30-day free trial that requires a credit card upfront. After the trial, you'll need to choose a paid plan to continue.

What's the cheapest Campaigner plan? ▼

The Starter plan at $59/month for up to 5,000 contacts is the cheapest entry point. However, if you're a Shopify or Magento store, the eCommerce plan starting at $79.95/month with unlimited contacts offers better value.

Is SMS really included in the Essential plan? ▼

Yes, the Essential plan ($179/month) includes 1,000 SMS sends per month with a free phone number, personalization, segmentation, and recurring campaigns. This is a genuine differentiator as most competitors charge extra for SMS.

Can I cancel my Campaigner subscription online? ▼

No, Campaigner requires a phone call to cancel. This is documented company policy to verify account ownership. If you're on an annual plan, cancellation mid-term may have additional conditions.

What's the difference between Starter and Essential? ▼

Essential adds dynamic content, multivariate testing, and 1,000 SMS sends per month. It also increases contact limits from 5,000 to 25,000. The price jumps from $59 to $179 — a significant step, but you get substantially more functionality.

Does Campaigner charge for overages? ▼

Campaigner uses subscriber-based pricing, not send-volume pricing. You pay for your contact count, not how many emails you send. However, SMS overages beyond your monthly limit incur per-message fees.
